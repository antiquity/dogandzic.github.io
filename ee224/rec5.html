Practice

Compute Fourier coefficients

A periodic CT signal $x(t)$ is defined over one period as \[ x(t) = \begin{cases} 1, & \text{for $0 < t < 0.7$ } \\ -1, & \text{for $0.7 < t < 0.8$ } \\ \end{cases} \] where the fundamental period of $x(t)$ is $T_0 = 0.8$s.

(a) Sketch $x(t)$ over the entire range $–1 \leqslant t \leqslant 1$s.

(b) What is the fundamental frequency of $x(t)$?

(c) Write the integral expression for the Fourier coefficients $a_k$ of the signal $x(t)$.

Determine signal from Fourier coefficients

A signal $x(t)$ is periodic with fundamental period $T_0 = 8$s and can be represented using Fourier series as \[ x(t) = \sum_{k=-\infty}^{+\infty} a_k \E^{\, \J k \omega_0 t } \] where $a_k$ are its Fourier coefficients.

(a) What is the fundamental frequency $\omega_0$ of $x(t)$?

(b) It is known that the Fourier coefficients $a_k$ for this $x(t)$ are given by the integrals \[ a_k = \frac{1}{8} \int_{-4}^{0} (4+t) \E^{ - \J k \omega_0 t } \dif t \] where $\omega_0$ is the fundamental frequency. Determine an equation for $x(t)$ that is valid over one period.

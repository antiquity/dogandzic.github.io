2. Linear versus circular convolution.

Consider two finite-length sequences $x_1[n]$ and $x_2[n]$ for which $x_1[n] = 0$ outside the interval $0 \leqslant n \leqslant 49$ and $x_2[n] = 0$ outside the interval $0 \leqslant n \leqslant 9$.

(a) What is the maximum possible number of nonzero values in the linear convolution of $x_1[n]$ and $x_2[n]$?

(b) The $50$-point circular convolution of $x_1[n]$ and $x_2[n]$ is $$x_1[n] \circledast_{50} x_2[n] = \{ 10, \,\, 0 \leqslant n \leqslant 49 \}.$$

The first five points of the linear convolution $\star$ of $x_1[n]$ and $x_2[n]$ are \[ y[n] \big|_{n=0,1,2,3,4} = ( x_1[n] \star x_2[n] ) \big|_{n=0,1,2,3,4} = 5. \] Determine as many points as possible of the linear convolution $y[n]= x_1[n] \star x_2[n]$.

3. Zero padding

A length-$N$ signal $x[n], \, n=0,1,\dotsc,N–1$ is zero-padded to length $2 N$, yielding \[x_{\text{a}}[n] = \underbrace{ \\{ x[0], x[1], \ldots, x[N-1], 0, 0, \ldots, 0 \\} }_{ \text{length $2 N$}},\]

and the corresponding DFT \(X_{\text{a}}^\td[k],\,k=0,1,\ldots,2 N - 1\) is computed. The length-$N$ inverse DFT of the sequence of odd-numbered terms $X_{\text{a}}^\td[2 l + 1]$ (for all $l$ such that $2 l + 1 \in {0,1,\ldots,2 N - 1}$) is then computed. What is the result of this operation?

Express the odd-numbered terms $X_{\text{a}}^\td[2 l + 1]$, $l$ integer, as a length-$N$ DFT of a certain sequence. Recall also the identity \(W_N^n = W_{N/n}\).
